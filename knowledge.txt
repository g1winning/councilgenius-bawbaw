[PAYING YOUR RATES]
Your rates notice will outline the amount of general rates and any service charges payable on your property. If you have a query about your rates notice, you can contact Council and we will be happy to help.

How to Pay:
- Online via the Customer Portal
- Credit card (Visa or Mastercard)
- Direct debit arrangement
- In person at Customer Service Centres (Warragul or Drouin)
- By mail to Baw Baw Shire Council, P.O. Box 304, Warragul 3820
- By phone on (03) 5624 2411 or 1300 BAW BAW (1300 229 229) during business hours

Payment Options:
- Pay in full
- By instalments – four equal instalments are available. The first instalment is due on the first date specified on your rates notice. Subsequent instalments are due on the same date each quarter.
- If you are having difficulty paying your rates, please contact Council to discuss your circumstances.

Rates Concessions:
If you are eligible, you may be able to apply for a rates concession. Eligible holders of Victorian Pensioner Concession Cards or Department of Veterans' Affairs Cards may qualify.

Acknowledging Payments:
Once your payment has been received and processed, you will be issued with a receipt. This can either be printed, emailed or posted to you depending on your preferred payment method.

Early Payment Discount:
Not available to Baw Baw Shire ratepayers.

Interest on Overdue Rates:
If your rates are not paid by the due date, interest will be charged at the prescribed rate set by the State Government (currently 11% per annum).

What Happens if Rates are Not Paid:
If rates remain unpaid after the first and second notice are issued, further action may be taken, which could include:
- Legal proceedings
- Suspension of permits
- Recovery of costs associated with taking action

We encourage ratepayers to contact Council if you are having difficulty paying your rates. We can discuss payment options and arrangements that may suit your circumstances.

[RATES VALUATION INFORMATION]
Rates are based on the Capital Improved Value (CIV) of your property, which is determined by the Valuer-General Victoria. If you believe your property valuation is incorrect, you have the right to object within 2 months of receiving your rates notice. Contact the Valuer-General Victoria directly for objection procedures.

[BUILDING PERMITS]
A building permit is required for most building work in Victoria. The Building Code of Australia sets out the building standards that must be met. Before any building work starts, you must obtain a building permit from Council or an approved private building surveyor.

Do I Need a Building Permit:
You will need to apply for a building permit if your building work includes:
- New buildings
- Additions or extensions (including decks and pergolas over certain heights or areas)
- Alterations to the structure
- Change of use or occupancy
- Roof coverings
- Installation of a swimming pool and safety barriers
- Building work on restricted land (e.g. land in a flood or bushfire zone)

Some minor building work may be exempt from requiring a building permit. If you are unsure, contact Council on (03) 5624 2411.

Applying for a Building Permit:
You can apply for a building permit by:
- Submitting an application online through the Customer Portal
- Visiting a Customer Service Centre
- Sending an application by post to Baw Baw Shire Council, P.O. Box 304, Warragul 3820

For more information on how to apply and the required documentation, visit the Building Services page.

Private Building Surveyors:
You don't have to use Council to assess your building permit application. You can engage a private building surveyor to process your application and inspect your work. Private building surveyors must be registered with the Victorian Building Authority and have professional indemnity insurance.

Building Permit Fees:
Building permit fees are based on the estimated cost of the building work. For current fees and charges, visit the Building Services Fees page.

Swimming Pool and Spa Barrier Registration:
All swimming pools and spas must have compliant safety barriers and be registered with Council. For more information, visit the Swimming Pool and Spa Barrier Registration page.

SOURCE_URL: www.bawbawshire.vic.gov.au/Plan-and-Build/Building/Building-Permits

[DOG REGISTRATION & RESPONSIBILITIES]
All dogs in Victoria must be registered with Council by the time they reach 3 months of age. Registration is compulsory under the Domestic Animals Act 2001.

How to Register Your Dog:
You can register your dog:
- Online through the Customer Portal
- In person at a Customer Service Centre (Warragul or Drouin)
- By mail to Baw Baw Shire Council, P.O. Box 304, Warragul 3820
- By phone on (03) 5624 2411

Registration Fees:
Current registration fees can be found on the Fees and Charges page. Discounts may be available for:
- Pensioners
- Dogs that have been desexed
- Assistance dogs

Annual Renewal:
Dog registration must be renewed annually. You will receive a renewal notice in the mail. Failure to renew registration is an offense and may result in a fine.

Changing Details:
If your dog's details change (e.g. change of address, new owner), you must notify Council within 14 days.

Responsibilities of Dog Owners:
As a responsible dog owner, you must:
- Keep your dog under control at all times
- Not allow your dog to attack, bite or chase another person or animal
- Keep your dog confined to your property
- Not allow your dog to cause a nuisance (e.g. excessive barking)
- Clean up after your dog in public places
- Ensure your dog is microchipped
- Comply with local law requirements

Dangerous, Menacing and Restricted Breed Dogs:
If you own a dog that falls into a dangerous, menacing or restricted breed category, you must comply with additional requirements. For more information, visit the Dangerous, Menacing and Restricted Breed Dogs page.

[ABOUT WASTE & RECYCLING]
Baw Baw Shire is committed to sustainable waste management and reducing landfill. We provide a range of waste and recycling services to residents.

Bins and Collections:
- General Rubbish Bin - Collected fortnightly
- Glass Recycling Bin - Collected fortnightly
- Mixed Recycling Bin - Collected fortnightly
- Garden Organics Bin - Collected fortnightly

Find Your Bin Collection Calendar:
Use the Baw Baw Waste App or visit the website to find your bin collection day. Collections occur on the same day each week. Please place bins out on collection day before 6am and remove them from the street by 6pm the same day.

The Baw Baw Waste App:
Download the app to receive notifications about your collection day, report missed collections, and access waste management information.

What Goes in Each Bin:

General Waste Bin:
- Non-recyclable items (plastic film, polystyrene)
- Items that won't decompose (ceramics)
- Worn-out clothing and textiles
- Small hard waste items

Recycling Bin:
- Paper and cardboard (newspapers, magazines, boxes, office paper)
- Cardboard: flatten large boxes to fit in bin
- Glass bottles and jars (rinsed, lids removed)
- Metal containers (tins, cans, aluminium foil)
- Plastic bottles and hard plastic containers #1-#7 (rinsed)
- NOT: plastic bags, plastic wrap, polystyrene, kitchen items

Garden Organics Bin:
- Grass clippings and weeds, free from soil
- Garden prunings including rose clippings
- Leaves
- Small logs and raw timber off-cuts up to 10cm wide and 30cm long
- NOT: bricks, stones, tree stumps, treated timber, noxious weeds

[SPECIALIZED WASTE COLLECTIONS]
The following items can be disposed of at Council's transfer stations:
- Hard waste
- Green waste
- E-waste and batteries
- Glass
- Timber
- Metal

Trafalgar Transfer Station

[E-WASTE & BATTERIES]
E-waste is no longer accepted in any waste bin. E-waste and batteries pose a fire risk for waste collection vehicles and processing facilities.

What is Electronic Waste (E-waste)?
Any unwanted electronic item with a plug, battery or cord including:
- Computers and office equipment
- Mobile phones
- TVs
- Microwaves
- Power tools
- Light bulbs

The Victorian Government banned e-waste from landfills in July 2019. E-waste cannot be placed in your kerbside bins.

Where to Take Your E-waste (including batteries):
- Council's transfer stations accept e-waste (including household and car batteries) free of charge
- Call the transfer station before bringing your E-waste if you have a large number of items to ensure they have capacity
- Drouin Civic Centre has an E-waste drop off point that accepts smaller E-waste items

[COMMINGLED RECYCLING]
Your kerbside commingled recycling collection helps recover resources and reduce landfill.

What is Contamination?
Contamination is any item that isn't on the Acceptable Recycling list. Some items, even if they have a recycling symbol, are unsuitable for the sorting process and can cause damage to the equipment.

Acceptable recycling:
- Cans, glass and cartons (bottle tops to be placed in the garbage)
- Paper and cardboard
- Hard plastic packaging, excluding expandable polystyrene (APES)
- Aerosol cans
- Pizza boxes (no food waste)

Unacceptable recycling:
- Rubbish
- Plastic bags, plastic wrap or bin liners
- Nappies or syringes
- Pyrex, window glass, light globes, broken glass or ceramics
- Green organics
- Waxed cardboard
- Polystyrene
- Garbage or food waste
- Hard waste, timber or building materials
- Car parts
- Clothing or carpet

[HOME COMPOSTING]
Composting is a great way to recycle naturally and help cut down on waste going to landfill.

Steps to Composting:
- Put your bin in a level, well-drained, sunny (but not too hot) position
- Start filling up the compost bin with the right ingredients
- Keep the compost free of pet droppings, meat scraps and bones, diseased plants and weeds
- Keep the ingredients balanced
- Use your compost when it is a dark brown, almost black soil-like material

Council may offer rebates or support for home composting initiatives. Contact Council for details.

[GLASS RECYCLING]
Council now has the solution to separate and recycle glass in dedicated skip bins at selected Transfer Stations.

Acceptable glass:
- Glass wine and beer bottles of all colours (with lids removed)
- Glass food and drink bottles of all colours (with lids removed)
- Glass jars and containers (with lids removed)

[SINGLE-USE PLASTICS BAN]
From 1 February 2023, problematic single-use plastics are banned from sale or supply in Victoria.

Single-use plastic ban items include:
- Plastic straws
- Plastic cutlery and plates
- Drink stirrers
- Expanded polystyrene food and drink containers
- Cotton buds

Residents should avoid purchasing these items where possible and seek alternative products.

[TEXTILE RECYCLING]

- In a school crossing zone
- Too close to an intersection
- Across a lane or private driveway
- Leaving less than 3 metres of road clear for traffic
- On a footpath, nature strip or reservation
- In a no stopping zone
- In a clearway, bus stop/zone
- Opposite continuous double white lines

SECTION 16: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
================================================================================

[FAQ - RATES & PAYMENTS]

Q: When are my rates due?
A: Rates instalments are due quarterly on dates specified on your rates notice. You can pay in full or by four equal instalments.

Q: Can I pay my rates online?
A: Yes, you can pay online through the Customer Portal, by BPay, or via our website. You can also pay by phone, mail, or in person.

Q: What happens if I pay late?
A: If rates are not paid by the due date, interest accrues at the prescribed rate (currently 11% per annum).

Q: Can I get a rates concession?
A: If you hold a Victorian Pensioner Concession Card or Department of Veterans' Affairs Card, you may be eligible for a rates concession.

Q: Who sets property valuations?
A: The Valuer-General Victoria (state government agency) sets all property valuations based on Capital Improved Value.

Q: How do I object to my property valuation?
A: You can object to the Valuer-General Victoria within 2 months of your rates notice. The objection is free to lodge.

[FAQ - WASTE & RECYCLING]

Q: When are my bins collected?
A: Use the Baw Baw Waste App or website to find your specific collection day. Collections occur fortnightly on the same day each week.

Q: What can I put in my recycling bin?
A: Paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, glass, and metal containers. Avoid plastic bags, polystyrene, and food contamination.

Q: Can I put e-waste in my bin?
A: No. E-waste must be taken to a transfer station or drop-off point due to fire safety concerns.

Q: What if my bin wasn't collected?
A: Report it online through the Council website or phone (03) 5624 2411. We typically respond within 2-3 working days.

Q: How do I get additional bins?
A: Apply online or contact Council. Additional bins may incur a fee.

Q: What are transfer station hours?
A: Hours vary by location. Lardner is daily 10am-4pm. Trafalgar is Sat/Sun/Mon 10am-4pm. Neerim South is Fri/Sun 10am-4pm. Erica is Mon/Wed/Sun 12pm-4pm.

[FAQ - PLANNING & BUILDING]

Q: Do I need a planning permit?
A: Depends on your proposal. Check VicPlan online or contact Council planning team for free advice.

Q: How long does planning approval take?
A: Standard applications take 60 days from receipt of a complete application.

Q: What is an RFI?
A: A Request for Further Information sent by Council when additional details are needed. You must respond within the deadline or your application may lapse.

Q: Do I need a building permit?
A: Most building work requires a building permit. Check with the Victorian Building Authority if unsure.

Q: Can I use a private building surveyor?
A: Yes. Private surveyors must be registered with the Victorian Building Authority.

Q: What happens if I build without permits?
A: You may be required to remove the work, pay fines, and obtain retroactive permits if possible.

[FAQ - ANIMALS & PETS]

Q: When do I need to register my dog?
A: By the time your dog reaches 3 months of age. Annual renewal is required.

Q: How much does dog registration cost?
A: Check the Fees and Charges page for current fees. Discounts available for pensioners, desexed dogs, and assistance dogs.

Q: What if I don't register my dog?
A: You'll receive a fine (currently not exceeding a significant amount). Failure to pay can result in further action.

Q: Must my dog be microchipped?
A: Yes. All registered dogs must be microchipped and wear a registration tag.

Q: Can I have more dogs than the residential limit?
A: No, without Council approval. Application required to vary the standard limit (usually 2 dogs per property).

Q: Are roosters allowed in residential areas?
A: No. Roosters are prohibited in residential zones. Check with Council if in rural areas.

[FAQ - LIBRARIES]

Q: Is library membership free?
A: Yes. Membership is free for Baw Baw Shire residents with proof of identity and address.

Q: How long can I borrow books?
A: Standard loan period is 28 days for books, 14 days for DVDs and magazines.

Q: What about overdue fines?
A: Overdue fees apply. Items can be renewed if not requested by others.

Q: Can I use the computer at the library?
A: Yes. Free computer access available during library opening hours.

Q: Do you have WiFi?
A: Yes. Free WiFi is available at all library locations.

[FAQ - SWIMMING]

Q: What are the pool opening hours?
A: Contact your local pool directly. Warragul (5623 3000), Drouin (5625 2394), Trafalgar (5633 1882).

Q: Do you offer swimming lessons?
A: Yes. All pools offer swimming lessons and programs. Contact your local pool for details.

Q: Is there an entry fee?
A: Yes. Contact the pool directly for current entry fees and pricing.

Q: Are children supervised at pools?
A: Pools have lifeguards on duty during operating hours. Children must be supervised by an adult at all times.

[FAQ - PARKING]

Q: Is parking free in Baw Baw Shire?
A: Most on-street parking is free. Some areas have time restrictions or permit requirements.

Q: How do I get a disabled parking permit?
A: Contact Council with proof of eligibility. Eligible holders include those with disability cards.

Q: What if I get a parking fine?
A: If you believe the fine is incorrect, you can request a review or challenge it through VCAT.

Q: Can I park on the nature strip?
A: No. Parking on nature strips is prohibited.

[FAQ - EMERGENCY & SAFETY]

Q: How do I sign up for emergency alerts?
A: Register on the Emergency Alert Service website or contact Council for details.

Q: What should I do in a bushfire?
A: Monitor the CFA website, know your evacuation routes, and leave early if conditions worsen.

Q: How do I prepare for an emergency?
A: Create an emergency plan, prepare an emergency kit, and discuss procedures with family.

Q: Who manages bushfires in my area?
A: Country Fire Authority (CFA) manages fire suppression. Council assists with planning and preparedness.

[FAQ - COMMUNITY & GENERAL]

Q: How do I find information about events?
A: Visit the Council website or call (03) 5624 2411 for information about community events.

Q: Can I apply for a community grant?
A: Yes. Community Grants Program provides funding to not-for-profit groups. Check the website for current rounds.

Q: How do I access Council meeting agendas?
A: Agendas and minutes are available on the Council website prior to and after meetings.

Q: Can I make a complaint?
A: Yes. You can lodge a formal complaint with Council. Contact the Customer Service team for the process.

Q: How do I request information?
A: Submit Freedom of Information requests through the Council website or contact Council directly.

Q: What languages is information available in?
A: Council offers translation services. Contact (03) 5624 2411 for information in other languages.

SECTION 23: ADVANCED PLANNING & DEVELOPMENT INFORMATION
================================================================================

[DETAILED PLANNING REQUIREMENTS]

Urban Growth Boundary:
Baw Baw Shire manages urban growth through planning controls:
- Urban Growth Boundary defines areas for residential expansion
- Growth areas have specific planning requirements
- Infrastructure planning for growth areas
- Heritage protection in established areas

Zone Information:

Residential Zone:
- Primary purpose: Residential housing
- Permitted uses: Houses, units, townhouses
- Planning permit required for: Multi-unit development, businesses, large buildings
- Setbacks and height restrictions apply
- Parking requirements enforced

General Residential Zone:
- Similar to Residential Zone
- Slightly different permit thresholds
- Application depends on specific location

Rural Zone:
- Primary purpose: Agricultural and farming use
- Permitted uses: Farms, orchards, hobby farms
- Residential houses on farmland allowed with conditions
- Large developments require planning approval
- Environmental controls apply

Agricultural Zone:
- Primary purpose: Productive agricultural land
- Restrictions on subdivision and dwellings
- Protection for farming operations
- Environmental overlays may apply

Business Zone:
- Primary purpose: Retail and business operations
- Permitted uses: Shops, offices, services
- Residential above ground floor sometimes allowed
- Parking and traffic requirements strict
- Design standards for facades and streetscape

Industrial Zone:
- Primary purpose: Manufacturing and industrial operations
- Permitted uses: Factories, warehouses, heavy industry
- Buffer zones from residential areas
- Environmental controls for emissions and waste
- Truck and traffic management

Township Zone:
- Primary purpose: Smaller towns with mixed uses
- Permitted uses: Retail, residential, community facilities
- More flexible than other zones
- Protects character of smaller towns

Special Use Zone:
- Purpose: Specific sites with unique requirements
- Examples: Showgrounds, sporting facilities, specific developments
- Requirements vary by site

[OVERLAYS & RESTRICTIONS]

Environmental Significance Overlay (ESO):
Applied to areas of environmental importance:
- Native vegetation protection
- Habitat and species protection
- Water quality and waterway protection
- Requires planning approval for certain works

Heritage Overlay:
Applied to buildings and areas of cultural significance:
- Protects heritage buildings and landscapes
- External changes require planning approval
- Demolition generally not permitted
- Internal changes usually not controlled

Native Vegetation Protection Overlay (NVPO):
Protects significant native vegetation:
- Planning approval required before removal
- Clearing restrictions and conditions
- Offsetting requirements for removal

Vegetation Management Overlay (VMO):
Protects trees and vegetation:
- Tree removal permits required in some areas
- Minimum tree retention standards
- Landscaping and planting requirements

Wildfire Hazard Management Overlay (WHMO):
Applies to bushfire-prone areas:
- Building design standards for bushfire resistance
- Fuel management requirements
- Defensible space provisions
- Construction materials restrictions

[DEVELOPMENT CONTRIBUTIONS & LEVIES]

Development Contributions:
Developers may be required to contribute to:
- Road and transport infrastructure
- Water, sewerage and drainage
- Community facilities
- Open space acquisition
- Environmental protection

Section 173 Agreements:
Legal agreements between Council and property owners regarding:
- Use and development restrictions
- Ongoing management obligations
- Financial contributions
- Conditions on future use

[APPEALS & DISPUTES]

Planning Permit Appeals:
If your planning permit is refused or conditions imposed you can:
- Request reconsideration from Council
- Appeal to VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal)
- Provide additional information supporting your position
- Request meeting with planning officers

VCAT (Victorian Civil and Administrative Tribunal):
Independent dispute resolution body:
- Hears planning disputes and appeals
- Makes binding decisions
- Provides fair and impartial review
- Lodge appeals within specified timeframe

Building Disputes:
Building disputes resolved through:
- Victorian Building Authority complaints
- Building practitioners board
- VCAT if unresolved
- Court action as final resort

1. Avoid: Buy less, choose minimal packaging
2. Reduce: Use less material, buy quality lasting items

5. Recover: Energy from waste, composting
6. Disposal: Landfill as last resort

- Increased recycling rates

SECTION 26: DETAILED ANIMAL MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

[COMPREHENSIVE DOG MANAGEMENT]

Domestic Animals Act 2001:
Dogs in Victoria managed under:
- Mandatory registration requirements
- Owner responsibility provisions
- Dangerous dog controls
- Breed restrictions on dangerous breeds
- Penalties for non-compliance

Working Dogs on Farms:
Special provisions for working dogs:
- Different registration category
- Lower fees than pets
- Must be actively working
- Farmer certification required
- Still requires microchip and tags

[DOG BEHAVIOR & CONTROL]

Effective Control Definition:
"Effective control" means:
- Dog responds to owner's commands
- Dog doesn't chase, approach, or threaten
- Dog stays within owner's field of vision
- Dog remains in safe proximity
- Dog doesn't pose risk to public

Preventing Attacks:
Owners responsible for preventing attacks:
- Keep dog secure on property
- Use appropriate containment
- Supervise outside interactions
- Manage aggressive tendencies
- Obtain professional training if needed

Attacking Dogs:
If dog attacks person or animal:
- Contact emergency services (000) if injuries
- Contact Council immediately
- Incident report required
- Council may take enforcement action
- Insurance claims may apply

[DANGEROUS & RESTRICTED BREEDS]

Dangerous Dog Categories:
Dogs declared dangerous if:
- Seriously injured or killed person or animal
- Threat of serious injury
- Evidence of dangerous tendencies
- Owner failed to control dog

Menacing Dogs:
Dogs may be declared menacing if:
- Caused minor injuries
- Demonstrated aggressive behavior
- Threat without serious injury
- Multiple minor incidents

Restricted Breed Dogs:
Certain breeds have restrictions:
- American Pit Bull Terrier
- Perro de Presa Canario
- Japanese Tosa
- Dogo Argentino

Restrictions include:
- Compulsory microchipping
- Restrictive containment requirements
- Public liability insurance
- Special collar and tags
- Council registration and approval

[LOST & FOUND PETS]

Lost Dog Procedures:
If your dog goes missing:
1. Search neighborhood immediately
2. Contact Council: (03) 5624 2411
3. Call local veterinary clinics
4. Post on community Facebook pages
5. Contact animal shelters and rescues
6. Place lost dog advertisements
7. Notify neighbors and post signs

Found Dog Procedures:
If you find a dog:
1. Get it to safety (may be injured or scared)
2. Look for ID tag or collar
3. Take to nearest vet clinic for microchip check
4. Contact Council with details
5. Contact local vets with description
6. Hold dog safely until owner found

Council's Role:
Council hold found dogs for 8-day reclaim period:
- Owner can reclaim during this time
- Release fee applies
- Owner must pay any boarding costs
- Unregistered dog must be registered before release
- After 8 days, Council may rehome or refer to rescue

[BARKING DOG DISPUTES]

Excessive Barking:
If neighbor's dog barks excessively:
- Document incidents with dates/times
- Measure noise level if possible
- Discuss with neighbor first (if safe)
- Contact Council for advice
- Lodge formal complaint if unresolved

Council Investigation:
Council may investigate barking complaints:
- Respond to verified complaints
- Assess reasonableness of barking
- May issue notice requiring management
- Fine if continues after notice
- Escalation to court if needed

Dog Owner Responsibilities:
Prevent barking by:
- Appropriate training and socialization
- Adequate exercise and stimulation
- Managing anxiety and triggers
- Consulting behaviorist if needed
- Installing barrier/sound reduction

[CAT MANAGEMENT]

Cat Registration:
Cats over 3 months must be registered:
- Similar process to dog registration
- Annual renewal required
- Microchipping mandatory
- Registration tag required
- Fees apply (generally lower than dogs)

Cat Behavior:
Responsible cat ownership includes:
- Keeping cat confined to property
- Managing hunting of wildlife
- Controlling noise and disturbances
- Proper identification and tags
- Regular health checks

Wildlife Protection:
Cats may kill native wildlife:
- Keep cats indoors or in enclosed areas
- Bell on collar helps warn wildlife
- Don't let cat roam freely
- Protect native animals in your area
- Desexing important for population control

[OTHER ANIMALS]

Rabbits & Ferrets:
- Registration required in some circumstances
- Containment and welfare requirements
- Numbers limits in residential areas
- Proper housing and care standards

Poultry & Roosters:
- Chickens, ducks allowed in residential (up to 10)
- Roosters PROHIBITED in residential zones
- Rural areas may permit roosters
- Noise and welfare requirements apply
- Council approval needed for larger flocks

Bees:
- Registration with Agriculture Victoria required
- Neighbor consent needed (within 100m)
- Council permit required for 2+ hives
- Disease management responsibilities
- Proper hive maintenance

Birds & Budgies:
- Up to 50 domestic birds allowed
- Proper housing and care required
- Noise considerations for neighbors
- Containment to prevent escape

Horses & Livestock:
- Rural properties may keep horses and livestock
- Fencing and containment requirements
- Water and pasture standards
- Veterinary health requirements
- Planning permit may be needed
